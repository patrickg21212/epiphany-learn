## Content Notes

This is the **smart buyer's module** — practical, non-technical, directly valuable for anyone about to spend money on AI tools. The tone throughout:
- Respects the reader's intelligence — they're adults making real business decisions
- Skeptical without being cynical — AI tools can genuinely help, but due diligence matters
- Actionable: checklists, frameworks, and concrete questions they can use immediately
- No fear-mongering — empowering, not alarming

## Lesson Summaries

### Lesson 1: Do You Actually Need AI?
The automation vs. AI distinction. A 4-step decision framework for triaging any process. Side-by-side comparison table. Covers the hybrid implementation pattern (automation + AI in the same workflow). Uses `RevealCard` for decision steps and `Quiz` with 3 questions.

### Lesson 2: Red Flags in AI Vendor Pitches
8 classic red flags decoded (accuracy claims, "learns from your data," integrations, ROI guarantees, setup complexity, industry expertise, exclusivity, demo data). The demo manipulation playbook. Uses `RevealCard` for red flags, `DragDrop` for sorting vendor signals, and `Quiz` with 3 questions.

### Lesson 3: 12 Questions to Ask Before You Buy
The full buyer's checklist covering: problem definition, onboarding, pricing at scale, error handling, output ownership, data training policies, cancellation, uptime, references, integrations, roadmap commitments, and failure modes. Includes a printable 10-item pre-buy checklist. Uses `RevealCard` for each question and `Quiz` with 3 questions.

### Lesson 4: Pricing Models and Build vs. Buy
Covers per-seat, consumption, and flat-rate pricing with risks for each. Hybrid pricing and hidden overage charges. Total cost of ownership beyond the pricing page. Build vs. buy decision framework in 5 scenarios. Quick reference DragDrop for scenario classification. Uses `RevealCard` for pricing types and build/buy scenarios, `DragDrop` for scenario sort, and `Quiz` with 3 questions.
