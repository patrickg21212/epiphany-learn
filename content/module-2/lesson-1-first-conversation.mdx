# Your First Real Conversation with AI

![Your first AI conversation](/images/generated/m2l1-intro.png)

Picture your smartest friend — the one who went to school for everything, reads constantly, and will actually sit with you for hours answering questions without getting annoyed.

Now put that friend in a chat box.

That's basically what ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are. You type something. They type back. It's not more complicated than texting.

---

## What Are These Things, Anyway?

<Visual name="m2-chat-interfaces" />

ChatGPT is made by a company called OpenAI. Claude is made by Anthropic. Gemini is made by Google. They're all large language models — which is a fancy way of saying "a computer that learned to talk by reading an enormous amount of human writing."

They're not robots. They don't have feelings. But they've read so much — books, articles, code, recipes, legal briefs, Reddit threads, scientific papers — that they've gotten remarkably good at sounding helpful.

The important thing: **they're all free to try.** You don't need a credit card. You don't need to install anything.

---

## Where to Go Right Now

<Visual name="m2-where-to-go" />

- **ChatGPT:** [chat.openai.com](https://chat.openai.com) — free tier is solid, GPT-4o included
- **Claude:** [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) — often better at writing and nuanced questions
- **Gemini:** [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com) — connected to Google Search, great for research

Pick one. Any one. Make a free account. It takes two minutes.

## Your First Prompt

<Visual name="m2-first-prompt" />

Here's the thing people get wrong: they open the chat box and freeze. They think there's a right way to talk to AI.

There isn't. Just ask like you'd ask a knowledgeable friend.

**Try typing this right now:**

> "I've never used AI before. What's one surprisingly useful thing you can help me with?"

That's it. Hit enter. See what happens.

You'll probably get a thoughtful answer with a few ideas. Maybe it suggests help with emails. Maybe trip planning. Maybe explaining something confusing from a doctor's visit.

Now you're having a conversation.

---

## What an AI Conversation Actually Looks Like

<Visual name="m2-conversation-flow" />

Here's a real example. No special tricks, no technical knowledge — just someone figuring out they have a useful tool.

**Person:** I have to write a thank-you note to my kid's teacher for the end of the year. It feels awkward and I never know what to say.

**AI:** That's such a nice thing to do. Here's a simple version you can adjust:

*"Dear Ms. Rivera, I wanted to take a moment to thank you for the incredible care and patience you showed [child's name] this year. They came home excited about learning more days than I can count, and that's because of you. Wishing you a wonderful summer — you deserve it."*

Want me to make it longer, shorter, or add something specific?

**Person:** Can you add something about how she helped my kid get over being nervous about math?

**AI:** Of course — here's the updated version:

*"Dear Ms. Rivera, I wanted to take a moment to thank you for the incredible care and patience you showed [child's name] this year. At the start of the school year, math felt like a mountain to them — now they actually look forward to it. That shift happened because of you. Wishing you a wonderful summer — you deserve every second of it."*

---

<Visual name="m2-intro-tasks" />

That's the whole thing. It's a conversation. You go back and forth. You ask for changes. It adapts.

No special syntax. No commands to memorize. If you can text a friend, you can use AI. And once you get comfortable going back and forth like this, you'll start finding uses for it everywhere.
