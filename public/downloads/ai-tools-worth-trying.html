AI Tools Worth Trying

A curated list of free AI tools organized by what you actually want to do

Congratulations on completing Module 3. You earned this.

1 Writing & Editing

Tools that help you write better, faster, and with less effort.

ChatGPT chat.openai.com
Writes, edits, summarizes, and answers questions in plain English.
Free: GPT-4o mini unlimited; GPT-4o daily limits Best for: Drafting emails, rewriting awkward text, brainstorming
Claude claude.ai
Conversational AI that's especially good at long documents and nuanced writing.
Free: Limited daily messages on Claude Sonnet Best for: Summarizing long articles, writing that needs careful tone
Grammarly grammarly.com
Checks grammar, spelling, tone, and clarity as you type — anywhere on the web.
Free: Core grammar & spelling; tone detection in browser extension Best for: Proofreading emails and assignments before you hit send
QuillBot quillbot.com
Paraphrases, summarizes, and rewrites sentences in different styles.
Free: Paraphraser (125 words), summarizer (1,200 words), grammar check Best for: Rewriting for clarity, summarizing an article fast
Hemingway Editor hemingwayapp.com
Highlights sentences that are too long, passive voice, and hard-to-read phrases.
Free: Full editing in browser — no account required Best for: Making your writing easier to read for any audience
2 Image Creation

Turn a text description into a real image — no design skills needed.

Adobe Firefly firefly.adobe.com
Generates images from text descriptions using Adobe's AI.
Free: 25 generative credits per month; no subscription required Best for: Clean, professional-looking images without design skills
Microsoft Designer designer.microsoft.com
Generates images from text using DALL-E; also makes social media graphics.
Free: Generous daily boosts; free with a Microsoft account Best for: Quick social posts, greeting cards, or visual ideas — fast and easy
Canva AI canva.com
Generates images and designs from text inside Canva's full design tool.
Free: Limited AI image credits per month on the free plan Best for: Designing AND generating images in one place
Google ImageFX aitestkitchen.withgoogle.com
Generates high-quality images from text prompts using Google's AI.
Free: Free with a Google account Best for: Experimenting with creative image ideas — very accessible interface
3 Research & Learning

Find information faster and actually understand what you're reading.

Perplexity AI perplexity.ai
Answers questions like a search engine but with cited, summarized responses.
Free: Unlimited standard searches; limited Pro searches per day Best for: Researching topics and seeing where the information comes from
Google NotebookLM notebooklm.google.com
Lets you upload your own documents and then ask questions about them.
Free: Fully free with Google account; up to 50 sources per notebook Best for: Studying a book, understanding a long report, reviewing meeting notes
Consensus consensus.app
Searches academic research papers and summarizes what the science actually says.
Free: Limited searches per day; enough for most everyday questions Best for: Fact-checking health claims, understanding research without full papers
Explainpaper explainpaper.com
Upload a research paper and ask it to explain confusing parts in plain English.
Free: Basic explanations are free Best for: Understanding a study without a science background
4 Productivity & Organization

Work smarter. Let AI handle the scheduling, note-taking, and follow-through.

Notion AI notion.so
Adds AI writing, summarizing, and task assistance inside Notion's workspace.
Free: Notion itself is free; AI features have a limited free trial Best for: Notion users who want AI to help draft or summarize notes
Reclaim.ai reclaim.ai
Automatically schedules your tasks, habits, and focus time around your calendar.
Free: 1 calendar, basic task scheduling, and habit tracking Best for: Anyone who struggles to find time between meetings
Otter.ai otter.ai
Records meetings and conversations and turns them into searchable transcripts.
Free: 300 minutes of transcription per month; up to 3 audio imports Best for: Taking notes in meetings or lectures without missing anything
Mem mem.ai
AI-powered note-taking that automatically connects related notes over time.
Free: Basic notes are free; AI features require paid plan Best for: Heavy note-takers who want AI to surface connections
5 Video & Audio

Transcribe, edit, and create audio and video without the headache.

ElevenLabs elevenlabs.io
Converts text into realistic spoken audio in many voices and languages.
Free: 10,000 characters per month (~10 minutes of audio) Best for: Voiceovers, listening to articles read aloud, accessibility
Descript descript.com
Edit audio and video by editing the transcript — like editing a document.
Free: Up to 1 hour of transcription per month; watermark-free basic exports Best for: Podcasters and video creators who want to cut filler words fast
CapCut capcut.com
Video editing with AI features including auto-captions, background removal, and effects.
Free: Very generous — most features free; no watermark on most exports Best for: Short-form social media video editing — no experience needed
6 Presentations & Documents

Go from blank page to polished presentation in minutes.

Gamma gamma.app
Creates polished presentations, documents, and web pages from a text prompt.
Free: 400 AI credits on signup — enough for several full presentations Best for: Building a presentation from scratch in minutes
Beautiful.ai beautiful.ai
Smart slides that automatically adjust layout and design as you add content.
Free: Unlimited presentations with Beautiful.ai branding; basic templates Best for: Professional-looking slides without design work
ChatPDF chatpdf.com
Upload any PDF and ask it questions in plain English.
Free: 2 PDFs per day, up to 120 pages each; 50 questions per PDF Best for: Understanding contracts, summarizing manuals, reviewing reports
Slides AI slidesai.io
Turns text or a topic into a full Google Slides presentation automatically.
Free: 3 presentations per month Best for: Google Slides users who want AI to do the first draft
7 Coding & Technical

You don't need to be a developer to use these — they're great for beginners too.

GitHub Copilot github.com/features/copilot
Suggests code as you type, explains code in plain English, and fixes errors.
Free: Free for individuals — 2,000 code completions/month on free plan Best for: Beginners who want help understanding what they're typing
Replit AI replit.com
Browser-based coding environment with AI that helps you write and debug code.
Free: Free account with limited compute; AI features on free tier Best for: Trying coding without installing anything on your computer
Claude claude.ai
Explains code, helps debug, and walks beginners through technical concepts.
Free: Limited daily messages on the free plan Best for: Asking "what does this code do?" or "how do I fix this error?"
8 Fun & Creative

Just because it's AI doesn't mean it has to be serious. These are genuinely fun.

Suno suno.com
Generates full songs — lyrics, melody, instruments, vocals — from a text prompt.
Free: 50 credits per day (~10 songs); non-commercial use on free plan Best for: Creating birthday songs, experimenting with music, or just having fun
Character.ai character.ai
Chat with AI versions of fictional characters, historical figures, or custom personas.
Free: Fully free with an account; some wait times during peak hours Best for: Creative writing, practicing conversations, entertainment
Nightcafe nightcafe.studio
Generates art in different artistic styles from text descriptions.
Free: 5 free credits per day; earn more by participating in the community Best for: Exploring art styles — impressionist, oil painting, digital art, and more

A few things to keep in mind

  • Free tiers change. Companies adjust what they offer for free all the time. If something listed as free now costs money, that's normal — check the tool's website.
  • Your data matters. Most free tools use your inputs to improve their models. Avoid typing sensitive personal information (passwords, financial details, private medical info) into any AI tool.
  • AI makes mistakes. Every tool on this list will occasionally be wrong. Always double-check anything important.
  • You don't need to try them all. Pick one or two that match something you actually do. That's enough to get started.
