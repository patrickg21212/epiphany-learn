# AI Tools Worth Trying
### A Free Cheat Sheet for Real People — No Tech Experience Required

---

## Writing & Editing

**ChatGPT** — https://chat.openai.com
What it does: Writes, edits, summarizes, and answers questions in plain English.
Free tier: Unlimited access to GPT-4o mini; GPT-4o with daily usage limits.
Best for: Drafting emails, rewriting anything that sounds awkward, brainstorming ideas.

**Claude** — https://claude.ai
What it does: Conversational AI that's especially good at long documents and nuanced writing.
Free tier: Limited daily messages on Claude Sonnet.
Best for: Summarizing long articles or reports, writing that needs careful tone.

**Grammarly** — https://grammarly.com
What it does: Checks grammar, spelling, tone, and clarity as you type — anywhere on the web.
Free tier: Core grammar and spelling checks; tone detection is free in the browser extension.
Best for: Proofreading emails, school assignments, or anything before you hit send.

**QuillBot** — https://quillbot.com
What it does: Paraphrases, summarizes, and rewrites sentences in different styles.
Free tier: Paraphraser (up to 125 words at a time), summarizer (up to 1,200 words), grammar checker.
Best for: Rewriting something you wrote so it sounds cleaner, summarizing an article fast.

**Hemingway Editor** — https://hemingwayapp.com
What it does: Highlights sentences that are too long, passive voice, and hard-to-read phrases.
Free tier: Full editing experience in the browser — no account required.
Best for: Making your writing easier to read for any audience.

## Image Creation

**Adobe Firefly** — https://firefly.adobe.com
What it does: Generates images from text descriptions using Adobe's AI.
Free tier: 25 generative credits per month; no subscription required.
Best for: Creating clean, professional-looking images without any design skills.

**Microsoft Designer / Bing Image Creator** — https://designer.microsoft.com
What it does: Generates images from text using DALL-E; also makes social media graphics.
Free tier: Generous daily boosts for image creation; free with a Microsoft account.
Best for: Quick social posts, greeting cards, or visual ideas — fast and easy.

**Canva AI (Magic Media)** — https://canva.com
What it does: Generates images and designs from text inside Canva's full design tool.
Free tier: Limited AI image credits per month on the free Canva plan.
Best for: People who want to design AND generate images in one place.

**Google ImageFX** — https://aitestkitchen.withgoogle.com/tools/image-fx
What it does: Generates high-quality images from text prompts using Google's AI.
Free tier: Free with a Google account.
Best for: Experimenting with creative image ideas — very accessible interface.

## Research & Learning

**Perplexity AI** — https://perplexity.ai
What it does: Answers questions like a search engine but with cited, summarized responses.
Free tier: Unlimited standard searches; limited "Pro" searches per day.
Best for: Researching a topic quickly and seeing where the information actually comes from.

**Google NotebookLM** — https://notebooklm.google.com
What it does: Lets you upload your own documents and then ask questions about them.
Free tier: Fully free with a Google account; up to 50 sources per notebook.
Best for: Studying a book, understanding a long report, or reviewing meeting notes.

**Consensus** — https://consensus.app
What it does: Searches academic research papers and summarizes what the science actually says.
Free tier: Limited searches per day; enough to answer most everyday questions.
Best for: Fact-checking health claims, understanding research without reading full papers.

**Explainpaper** — https://explainpaper.com
What it does: Upload a research paper and ask it to explain confusing parts in plain English.
Free tier: Basic explanations are free.
Best for: Anyone who wants to understand a study or article without a science background.

## Productivity & Organization

**Notion AI** — https://notion.so
What it does: Adds AI writing, summarizing, and task assistance inside Notion's workspace.
Free tier: Notion itself is free; AI features have a limited free trial, then $10/month.
Best for: People already using Notion who want AI to help draft or summarize their notes.

**Reclaim.ai** — https://reclaim.ai
What it does: Automatically schedules your tasks, habits, and focus time around your calendar.
Free tier: Free plan covers 1 calendar, basic task scheduling, and habit tracking.
Best for: Anyone who struggles to find time for important work between meetings.

**Otter.ai** — https://otter.ai
What it does: Records meetings and conversations and turns them into searchable transcripts.
Free tier: 300 minutes of transcription per month; up to 3 audio imports.
Best for: Taking notes in meetings, interviews, or lectures without missing anything.

**Mem** — https://mem.ai
What it does: AI-powered note-taking that automatically connects related notes over time.
Free tier: Basic notes are free; AI features require a paid plan.
Best for: People who take a lot of notes and want AI to surface connections between them.

## Video & Audio

**Otter.ai** — https://otter.ai
What it does: Transcribes audio and video recordings into text automatically.
Free tier: 300 minutes/month; real-time transcription on mobile and desktop.
Best for: Turning recordings into searchable text — great for students and professionals.

**ElevenLabs** — https://elevenlabs.io
What it does: Converts text into realistic spoken audio in many voices and languages.
Free tier: 10,000 characters per month (roughly 10 minutes of audio).
Best for: Creating voiceovers, listening to articles read aloud, or accessibility uses.

**Descript** — https://descript.com
What it does: Edit audio and video by editing the transcript — like editing a document.
Free tier: Up to 1 hour of transcription per month; watermark-free export on basic edits.
Best for: Podcasters, video creators, or anyone who wants to cut "ums" from recordings.

**CapCut** — https://capcut.com
What it does: Video editing with AI features including auto-captions, background removal, and effects.
Free tier: Very generous — most features are free; no watermark on most exports.
Best for: Short-form video editing for social media, even with no editing experience.

## Presentations & Documents

**Gamma** — https://gamma.app
What it does: Creates polished presentations, documents, and web pages from a text prompt.
Free tier: 400 AI credits on signup (enough for several full presentations); unlimited views.
Best for: Building a presentation from scratch in minutes, without wrestling with slides.

**Beautiful.ai** — https://beautiful.ai
What it does: Smart slides that automatically adjust layout and design as you add content.
Free tier: Unlimited presentations with Beautiful.ai branding; basic templates.
Best for: People who want presentations that look professional without design work.

**ChatPDF** — https://chatpdf.com
What it does: Upload any PDF and ask it questions in plain English.
Free tier: 2 PDFs per day, up to 120 pages each; 50 questions per PDF.
Best for: Understanding a contract, summarizing a manual, or reviewing a long report.

**Slides AI** — https://slidesai.io
What it does: Turns text or a topic into a full Google Slides presentation automatically.
Free tier: 3 presentations per month.
Best for: Google Slides users who want AI to do the first draft.

## Coding & Technical

**GitHub Copilot** — https://github.com/features/copilot
What it does: Suggests code as you type, explains code in plain English, and fixes errors.
Free tier: Free for individual developers (as of 2025) — 2,000 code completions/month on free plan.
Best for: Beginners writing their first code who want help understanding what they're typing.

**Replit AI** — https://replit.com
What it does: Browser-based coding environment with AI that helps you write and debug code.
Free tier: Free account with limited compute; AI features available on free tier.
Best for: Someone who wants to try coding without installing anything on their computer.

**Claude** — https://claude.ai
What it does: Explains code, helps debug, and walks beginners through technical concepts.
Free tier: Limited daily messages on the free plan.
Best for: Asking "what does this code do?" or "how do I fix this error?" in plain English.

## Fun & Creative

**Suno** — https://suno.com
What it does: Generates full songs — lyrics, melody, instruments, vocals — from a text prompt.
Free tier: 50 credits per day (about 10 songs); non-commercial use only on free plan.
Best for: Creating a birthday song, experimenting with music, or just having fun.

**Character.ai** — https://character.ai
What it does: Chat with AI versions of fictional characters, historical figures, or custom personas.
Free tier: Fully free with an account; some wait times during peak hours.
Best for: Creative writing, practicing conversations, or entertainment.

**Nightcafe** — https://nightcafe.studio
What it does: Generates art in different artistic styles from text descriptions.
Free tier: 5 free credits per day; earn more by participating in the community.
Best for: Exploring different art styles — impressionist, oil painting, digital art, and more.

## A Few Things to Keep in Mind

- **Free tiers change.** Companies adjust what they offer for free all the time. If something listed as free now costs money, that's normal — check the tool's website.
- **Your data matters.** Most free tools use your inputs to improve their models. Avoid typing sensitive personal information (passwords, financial details, private medical info) into any AI tool.
- **AI makes mistakes.** Every tool on this list will occasionally be wrong. Always double-check anything important.
- **You don't need to try them all.** Pick one or two that match something you actually do. That's enough to get started.
